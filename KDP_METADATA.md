## Plain Description

Building Embodied AI is a hands-on, rigorous guide to building AI systems that perceive, decide, act, and learn in the physical or simulated world.

The book develops embodied intelligence as a full stack: agent-environment interfaces, spatial representation, kinematics, dynamics, control, sensors, simulation, reinforcement learning, imitation learning, robot data, perception, language grounding, vision-language-action models, world models, manipulation, locomotion, humanoids, multi-agent systems, human-robot interaction, safety, robustness, deployment, memory, continual learning, and capstone course design.

Each chapter connects theory to implementation. Readers move from first-principles models to runnable recipes, worked examples, tool choices, engineering tradeoffs, failure modes, exercises, and labs. The emphasis is practical without becoming shallow: the book explains the mathematics and assumptions behind the methods, then shows how to build with modern libraries and systems such as Gymnasium, PettingZoo, MuJoCo, MJX, Isaac Lab, Genesis, Drake, ROS 2, PyTorch, JAX, simulation benchmarks, robot datasets, and deployment tooling.

This volume is part of the Hands-On AI Science series, a collection of self-contained books designed for serious self-study, university courses, research seminars, and engineering teams that need both conceptual depth and build fluency.

Readers should have basic Python and machine learning background. The book supplies the robotics, control, simulation, and embodied learning foundations needed to continue.
