Building Embodied AI is a hands-on, rigorous guide to building AI systems that perceive, decide, act, and learn in the physical or simulated world. It is written for readers who want more than an API tour: the book develops the scientific foundations of embodied intelligence, then turns those foundations into runnable systems, practical recipes, evaluation methods, and course-ready labs.

Embodied AI changes the usual machine learning problem. A model no longer only predicts labels, tokens, or pixels. It must observe a scene, represent space, choose actions, handle dynamics, recover from failure, reason under uncertainty, and interact with consequences. This book treats embodied intelligence as a connected stack, from agent-environment interfaces and robot geometry to modern vision-language-action policies, simulation pipelines, safety, deployment, and continual learning.

The coverage includes spatial representation, coordinate frames, transformations, kinematics, dynamics, control, sensors, simulation, reinforcement learning, imitation learning, robot datasets, perception, language grounding, memory, planning, world models, manipulation, locomotion, humanoid systems, multi-agent embodied environments, human-robot interaction, robustness, monitoring, and evaluation. The book connects classical robotics and control to current AI practice without treating either side as optional.

Readers learn how to reason about:

  • How embodied agents turn observations into state, belief, memory, and action
  • How geometry, physics, control, and uncertainty shape learning systems
  • How reinforcement learning, imitation learning, offline learning, and model-based methods fit into robot learning
  • How simulation platforms, robot benchmarks, and datasets support development and evaluation
  • How vision-language-action models, robot foundation models, and world models are built and tested
  • How manipulation, locomotion, navigation, humanoid behavior, and multi-agent interaction differ in practice
  • How safety, sim-to-real transfer, deployment, monitoring, and failure analysis affect real embodied systems

Each chapter connects theory to implementation. Readers move from first-principles models to runnable recipes, worked examples, tool choices, engineering tradeoffs, failure modes, exercises, and labs. The emphasis is practical without becoming shallow: the book explains the mathematics and assumptions behind the methods, then shows how to build with modern libraries and systems such as Gymnasium, PettingZoo, MuJoCo, MJX, Isaac Lab, Genesis, Drake, ROS 2, PyTorch, JAX, simulation benchmarks, robot datasets, and deployment tooling.

The book is designed to support several paths through the material. Advanced students can use it as a structured introduction to embodied AI research. Engineers can use it as a practical map of architectures, tools, and debugging patterns. Instructors can build a serious AI or robotics course around the conceptual maps, worked examples, self-checks, labs, and capstone projects. Researchers from language, vision, reinforcement learning, or robotics can use it to connect their existing background to full embodied systems.

This volume is part of the Hands-On AI Science series, a collection of self-contained books designed for serious self-study, university courses, research seminars, and engineering teams that need both conceptual depth and build fluency.

Readers should have basic Python and machine learning background. The book supplies the robotics, control, simulation, and embodied learning foundations needed to continue, while giving enough practical detail to start building, testing, and improving embodied AI systems.
