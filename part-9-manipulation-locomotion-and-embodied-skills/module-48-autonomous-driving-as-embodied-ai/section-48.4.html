The core contract is: observe route graph, drivable area, dynamic obstacles, comfort, and rules, choose a trajectory that is feasible, legal, and socially legible, and judge the result with constraint violation, comfort, and time to collision margin.

Action Is The Test

Driving is embodied AI at road scale: the agent senses a partial world, predicts other agents, acts through vehicle dynamics, and carries social and safety obligations. For Route and local planning, the decisive question is whether the loop can recover from the planner finds a collision-free path that no controller can track smoothly.

Theory

The minimal formal object is a partially observed control loop. At time $t$, the agent receives $o_t$, estimates $\hat s_t$, chooses action $a_t$, and receives a new observation $o_{t+1}$. In Route and local planning, the important state variables are route graph, drivable area, dynamic obstacles, comfort, and rules, while the action interface is to choose a trajectory that is feasible, legal, and socially legible.

The method is useful only if it improves a construct-matched measurement: constraint violation, comfort, and time to collision margin. That metric must be co-computed for the baseline and the library shortcut on the same scenario panel, with the same seed policy and the same termination rules.

Mechanism

Driving planners must separate route choice, behavior selection, trajectory generation, and control feasibility. A route is not safe until the local plan satisfies road rules, dynamics, and actor predictions.
