The core contract is: observe video, map tokens, action history, latent dynamics, and route command, learn a policy or world model that predicts consequences before acting, and judge the result with closed-loop scenario success and recovery under distribution shift.

Action Is The Test

Driving is embodied AI at road scale: the agent senses a partial world, predicts other agents, acts through vehicle dynamics, and carries social and safety obligations. For End-to-end and world-model driving, the decisive question is whether the loop can recover from an end-to-end model looks smooth until it meets a rare scenario outside training coverage.

Theory

The minimal formal object is a partially observed control loop. At time $t$, the agent receives $o_t$, estimates $\hat s_t$, chooses action $a_t$, and receives a new observation $o_{t+1}$. In End-to-end and world-model driving, the important state variables are video, map tokens, action history, latent dynamics, and route command, while the action interface is to learn a policy or world model that predicts consequences before acting.

The method is useful only if it improves a construct-matched measurement: closed-loop scenario success and recovery under distribution shift. That metric must be co-computed for the baseline and the library shortcut on the same scenario panel, with the same seed policy and the same termination rules.

Mechanism

End-to-end driving still needs inspectable interfaces: observation window, latent state, predicted future, action proposal, safety monitor, and closed-loop replay. The model is not exempt from evidence.
