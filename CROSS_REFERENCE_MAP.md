# Embodied AI Cross-Reference Map

This map tracks concepts that recur across the book. Writers should use it when adding links, prerequisites, summaries, and `looking-back`, `whats-next`, or `cross-ref` callouts.

## Core Spine

### Perception-Action Loop

First introduced: Chapter 1

Deepened in: Chapters 2, 3, 27, 31, 34, 36, 52

Use whenever a chapter explains how sensing, representation, prediction, planning, control, action, and learning close the loop.

### State, Observation, and Partial Observability

First introduced: Chapter 2

Deepened in: Chapters 8, 14, 19, 29, 36, 53

Use when explaining why embodied agents rarely see the complete world state and must estimate hidden variables.

### Coordinate Frames and Geometry

First introduced: Chapter 4

Deepened in: Chapters 5, 8, 27, 28, 29, 30, 42, 46, 47, 48

Use when a method depends on poses, transforms, camera frames, robot frames, maps, or 3D reasoning.

### Control vs Policy

First introduced: Chapter 7

Deepened in: Chapters 15, 17, 20, 37, 45, 46, 54, 55

Use when comparing classical feedback controllers, learned policies, MPC, safety filters, and deployment control loops.

### Simulation and the Reality Gap

First introduced: Chapter 9

Deepened in: Chapters 11, 13, 17, 20, 44, 45, 46, 52

Use when simulated performance is compared with real-world behavior or when domain randomization, system identification, or sim-to-real transfer appears.
